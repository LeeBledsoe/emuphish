Learn About Phishing

Email phishing is an attempt by cybercriminals to steal your personal information by pretending to be a trusted source.

  • Types of Phishing: Spear Phishing, Clone Phishing, Whaling, etc.
  • How to Recognize: Spelling errors, suspicious links, and unusual requests.
  • Prevention Tips: Verify senders, don't click unknown links, and use spam filters.

Click-through Examples

Example 1: Suspicious Link

From: support@amaz0n.com

Subject: Confirm Your Account Now!

Click here to claim your reward: Claim Reward

Warning: The link points to a suspicious URL (http://fake-amazon-support.com)

Notice that the sender's address is slightly misspelled: support@amaz0n.com instead of support@amazon.com.

Example 2: Fake Invoice

From: billing@randomcompany.com

Subject: Invoice Due: Immediate Payment Required

Please find attached the invoice for your recent purchase. Click here to pay immediately: Pay Now

Warning: The link points to a suspicious URL (http://fake-invoice.com)

The sender's email address does not match any known billing department. Always verify the sender.

Example 3: Urgent Request for Payment

From: ceo@company.com

Subject: Urgent: Transfer Funds Immediately

We need you to transfer funds to the account below urgently. This is a confidential request, please do not discuss it with anyone.

This email creates a false sense of urgency and secrecy. Always verify such requests through direct communication.

Example 4: Verify Your Account

From: no-reply@securebank.com

Subject: Verify Your Account Information

We need to verify your account information. Please click the link below to continue: Verify Now

Warning: The link points to a suspicious URL (http://fake-securebank.com)

Banks typically do not ask for personal information via email. Verify by contacting your bank directly.
